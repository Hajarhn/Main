I'm a software person who landed in health research and ended up loving the overlap. My first degree was in software engineering; then medical informatics pulled me toward healthcare, and a PhD in data science kept me there. Today I work at Maastricht University Medical Centre+ on the data behind the Post-COVID Network Netherlands.

The thread running through all of it is making data usable — helping studies that were never designed to fit together actually work together, through careful harmonization, FAIR principles, and shared vocabularies. It's detailed, patient work, and I like that it quietly makes everyone else's research possible.

I'm also a builder at heart. When a spreadsheet stops being enough or a workflow gets painful, I'd rather build the tool than complain about the gap — from research software to small mobile apps. I care about tools that are honest about their limits and friendly to whoever uses them when I'm not in the room.
