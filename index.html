2026 · Post-COVID Network NL

PCNN Codebook Editor & Viewer

A single-file browser-based system for editing and publishing the codebook (data dictionary) of a national post-COVID research network. The internal editor handles 500+ variables across 20 questionnaires with auto-tracking, family-aware bulk updates, and copyright filtering. The public viewer lets researchers browse, search, and export — no install, no login, no patient data exposed.

I'm a software person who landed in health research and ended up loving the overlap. My background is in software; I work day-to-day at Maastricht University Medical Centre+ on the data infrastructure of the Post-COVID Network Netherlands. The tools I build live at the intersection of research workflows and software — the kind of thing that researchers need but no SaaS product quite fits.

The work I take on outside of MUMC+ is similar in shape: a small team or independent professional has a specific problem, usually messy data or a process that's grown beyond what a spreadsheet can hold. I prefer projects where the brief is clear, the people are kind, and the result will actually be used.

I care about clean tools — fast, honest about their limits, and friendly to whoever has to use them when I'm not in the room. Most of what I build is plain HTML, JavaScript, and a small database. Not because newer frameworks are bad, but because boring stacks outlive their authors.
